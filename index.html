ğŸ“‹ What To Do In An Emergency

Quick step-by-step guidance for common emergency situations.

ğŸš— Accident / Injury

  • Stay calm and assess the situation
  • Call emergency services immediately (112)
  • Do not move the injured person unless necessary
  • Apply basic first aid if trained
  • Keep the area safe from further accidents

ğŸ”¥ Fire Emergency

  • Alert everyone and evacuate immediately
  • Call Fire Brigade (101)
  • Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation
  • Never use elevators during fire
  • Meet at designated assembly point

ğŸ¥ Medical Emergency

  • Check if the person is responsive
  • Call ambulance (102 or 108)
  • Perform CPR if trained and needed
  • Keep the person comfortable
  • Note symptoms to tell medical staff

ğŸ©¹ Basic First Aid Tips

Essential first aid knowledge everyone should know.

ğŸ©¸ Minor Cuts & Bleeding

Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth. Elevate the injured area above heart level. Clean the wound gently and apply a bandage. Seek medical help if bleeding doesn't stop.

ğŸ”¥ Burns

Cool the burn under running cool water for at least 10 minutes. Do not apply ice, butter, or toothpaste. Cover with a clean, non-stick bandage. Seek medical help for severe burns.

ğŸ˜µ Fainting

Help the person lie down and elevate their legs. Loosen tight clothes. Ensure fresh air circulation. If they don't recover quickly, call for medical help.

ğŸ˜® Choking

Encourage coughing if the person can breathe. Perform back blows between shoulder blades. Use abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) if needed. Call emergency services if the object doesn't come out.
